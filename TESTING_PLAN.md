# YouthGuide NA - In-Field Testing Plan

## Overview
This document outlines the participatory testing approach for evaluating YouthGuide NA with unemployed youth in Havana, Windhoek.

---

## 🎯 Testing Objectives

1. **Usability**: Can youth easily navigate and complete tasks?
2. **Helpfulness**: Do suggestions feel relevant and trustworthy?
3. **Tone & Language**: Does the bot sound friendly, respectful, and local?
4. **Privacy Comfort**: Do users feel safe sharing information?
5. **Task Success**: Can users find opportunities that match their needs?

---

## 👥 Participant Recruitment

### Criteria
- Age: 16-35 years old
- Location: Havana, Windhoek
- Employment status: Unemployed or seeking opportunities
- Prior participation in workshop sessions (preferred)

### Sample Size
- **10 participants** (diverse age, gender, skills)

### Compensation
- N$50 airtime voucher per participant
- Transportation reimbursement if needed

---

## 📝 Testing Tasks

### Task 1: Find a Free Training Program
**Scenario**: "You want to learn a new skill but have no money. Find a free training course."

**Success criteria**:
- User finds at least one free training opportunity
- User understands how to apply/contact

**Time limit**: 3 minutes

---

### Task 2: Find a Part-Time Job
**Scenario**: "You need to earn money quickly. Find a part-time job you could start this week."

**Success criteria**:
- User finds a job listing
- User identifies pay rate and contact method

**Time limit**: 3 minutes

---

### Task 3: Save an Opportunity
**Scenario**: "You found something interesting. Save it so you can come back later."

**Success criteria**:
- User successfully saves an opportunity
- User can navigate to "Saved" and view it

**Time limit**: 2 minutes

---

### Task 4: Ask for Personalized Help
**Scenario**: "Tell the chatbot about your skills and interests. Ask it to suggest something that matches YOU."

**Success criteria**:
- User shares profile information
- Bot provides personalized suggestions
- User feels suggestions are relevant

**Time limit**: 4 minutes

---

### Task 5: Contact an Opportunity
**Scenario**: "Pick one opportunity you like. Find out how to contact them."

**Success criteria**:
- User identifies contact method (phone/WhatsApp/email)
- User understands next steps

**Time limit**: 2 minutes

---

## 📊 Metrics to Collect

### Quantitative Metrics

| Metric | How to Measure | Target |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| **Task Success Rate** | % of tasks completed successfully | ≥80% |
| **Time-to-Complete** | Average seconds per task | ≤3 min |
| **Save Rate** | % of users who save ≥1 opportunity | ≥70% |
| **Personalization Accuracy** | User rating of relevance (1-5) | ≥4.0 |
| **Error Rate** | # of navigation errors per session | ≤2 |

### Qualitative Metrics

**Post-Task Survey** (1-5 scale):

1. **Ease of Use**: "How easy was it to use YouthGuide NA?"
2. **Helpfulness**: "How helpful were the suggestions?"
3. **Trustworthiness**: "Do you trust the information provided?"
4. **Tone/Language**: "Did the chatbot feel friendly and respectful?"
5. **Privacy Comfort**: "Did you feel comfortable sharing your information?"
6. **Likelihood to Use Again**: "Would you use this again to find opportunities?"

**Open-Ended Questions**:
- What did you like most about YouthGuide NA?
- What was confusing or difficult?
- What would you change or add?
- Would you recommend this to a friend? Why or why not?

## 🛠️ Testing Setup

### Materials Needed
- 10 smartphones (Android, low-mid range)
- Testing script (printed)
- Consent forms (privacy & recording)
- Post-task survey (printed or Google Form)
- Notebook for observations
- Audio recorder (with consent)

### Environment
- Quiet space (community center or youth office)
- Stable Wi-Fi or mobile data
- 1-on-1 sessions (30-45 min each)

---

## 📋 Testing Protocol

### Before Session (5 min)
1. Welcome participant
2. Explain research purpose
3. Obtain informed consent (privacy, recording, data use)
4. Brief demo: "This is a chatbot that helps you find jobs and training. Try it out!"

### During Session (30 min)
1. **Task 1-5**: Present scenario, observe, take notes
2. **Think-Aloud**: Encourage participant to verbalize thoughts
3. **No help unless stuck**: Let them struggle a bit to identify issues
4. **Record screen** (with permission)

### After Session (10 min)
1. **Post-task survey** (quantitative + open-ended)
2. **Thank participant** and provide compensation
3. **Debrief**: Any additional feedback?

---

## 📈 Data Analysis

### Quantitative Analysis
- Calculate success rates, average times, ratings
- Identify patterns (e.g., which tasks had lowest success?)

### Qualitative Analysis
- Thematic coding of open-ended responses
- Identify common pain points (e.g., "unclear how to contact")
- Note positive feedback and feature requests

### Bias Audit
- Compare metrics across:
  - Gender (male/female/other)
  - Age brackets (16-20, 21-25, 26-30, 31-35)
  - Skills categories (manual labor vs. ICT)
- Check for disparities in satisfaction or success rates

---

## 🔄 Iteration Plan

### Issues to Watch For
1. **Low task success rate** → Simplify UI or add onboarding
2. **Long completion times** → Reduce steps or add shortcuts
3. **Low trust ratings** → Emphasize source verification
4. **Poor personalization** → Improve RAG retrieval or profile questions
5. **Privacy concerns** → Add clearer consent and data deletion options

### Post-Testing Actions
1. **Compile report** with findings and recommendations
2. **Prioritize fixes** based on severity and frequency
3. **Implement changes** (2-week sprint)
4. **Optional second round** with 5 users to validate fixes

---

## 📄 Ethical Considerations

### Privacy
- Only collect minimal PII (first name, age bracket)
- Anonymize all data (use participant IDs, not names)
- Store data securely (encrypted, password-protected)
- Delete data after research (or provide opt-in for retention)

### Consent
- Written consent before testing
- Explain data use clearly
- Right to withdraw at any time (no penalty)

### Compensation
- Fair compensation (N$50 airtime)
- Provided even if participant withdraws

---

## 📌 Deliverables

   - Task success rates
   - Survey results (quantitative + qualitative)
   - Key insights and recommendations

3. **Iteration Roadmap** (prioritized fixes)
